Coffee cultivation began in Yemen in the 15th century, with roots in Ethiopia, before spreading through the Arabian Peninsula to Europe and the rest of the world.

Two main species, Coffea arabica (milder, aromatic) and Coffea canephora (Robusta, stronger)

Coffeehouses are central to social life and business.

Ethiopian coffee ceremony highlighting its cultural significance.

It can be served in many ways, including espresso, French press, and lattes.
